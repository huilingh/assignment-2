Beijing Home Price Analysis (2011-2017)¶

For this assignment, I am using the Beijing home price data (2011-2017). The original dataset is downloaded from Kaggle https://www.kaggle.com/ruiqurm/lianjia, and I have already modified this dataset by adding the following fields:

  1. "district_name": unfortunately the "district" column in the original dataset does not show the names of the districts. I have figured out those names and added them in the data, so it makes more sense to people rather than just district numbers.

  2. "Lng_transed" and "Lat_transed": these two fields will not be used in this analysis, though.

The two charts above are very interesting - they are highly consistent. The monthly average home price reached its peak in March and April, and then kept going down to the end of 2017. And the average DOM was changing synchronously and negatively correlated with home price. These two charts together show that the housing market in Beijing was actually going down in 2017.

2. Seaborn Chart¶

Out[7]:

It seems like the home price (per square meter) is following a skewed normal distribution, while the construction time is more discrete and changing periodically. The relationship between price and construction time is not very clear on the chart, but it looks like they are slightly negatively correlated. I wouldn't be surprised of that, since houses constructed in early years are mostly located in center city. Beijing has been through a vast urban sprawl in the past 30 years, and housing price of old buildings can be extremely high due to their great locations.
